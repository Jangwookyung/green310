The Gallery of the Acropolis Slopes

The first exhibition space, on the Museum’s ground floor, contains finds from the Acropolis slopes. Representing an integral part of the Rock's topography and history, the slopes hosted small and large sanctuaries, old and new cults, theatrical and musical venues and, further downslope, private residences.

  • Archaic Acropolis Gallery

    The first floor’s eastern and southern sections are devoted to the Acropolis during the Archaic period.

  • The Parthenon Gallery

    The Museum's exhibition culminates in the third-floor gallery, which has been specially designed to accommodate the sculptures of the Parthenon.

ACTIVITIES

  • The exhibits of the Acropolis Museum, as well as those which it houses on loan from time to time, tell their own unique stories through a variety of permanent and time limited activities organized in its spaces.

  • Are you planning to visit the Acropolis Museum with your students? Choose one of the educational programs and follow our archaeologists in a unique travel through time, art, myth, history and everyday life of the ancient Athenians.

  • The Acropolis Museum offers festivities and family summer programs (also occasionally during winter months) that include exhibits and workshops specifically for children.

  • The Acropolis Museum, as a cultural institution dedicated in serving the public, implements social responsibility actions and programs oriented toward special groups of visitors in both physical presence and online.
